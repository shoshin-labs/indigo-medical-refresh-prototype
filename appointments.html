Appointments page redesign

Make the appointment route clear in under 10 seconds.

Instead of leading with long explanatory paragraphs, this version turns the page into a simple decision framework: routine, urgent, online, or out-of-hours.

If you believe the problem is life-threatening, call 999 immediately. For urgent same-day practice help, call the surgery directly rather than using an online form.

Choose the right route

Patients should not have to interpret policy text before they know what to do next.

Routine appointment

For a standard GP or nurse appointment, call reception or visit the surgery. The refreshed design keeps this clear and prominent at the top.

Call reception

Urgent same-day problem

If the issue will not wait for the next routine appointment, phone the surgery and tell the receptionist it is urgent so the team can triage appropriately.

Urgent contact guidance

Online booking

Explain clearly that Patient Access supports book-on-the-day appointments from 8am, and link directly to a simple onboarding page.

See Patient Access steps

Routine consultations

Offer patients a quick explanation of consultation formats, such as in person or by telephone, without forcing them to read multiple paragraphs first.

Home visits

Reserve for housebound or clinically appropriate cases, with the request route and time expectation stated clearly.

Out of hours

Move emergency and after-hours advice into a more visible summary block rather than leaving it buried lower down the page.

Recommended content pattern

Lead with actions, then explain the detail underneath.

Operational detail still matters, but it works better when layered below the first decision. This structure allows the practice to remain careful and clinically responsible while being easier for patients to use.

  • Top of page: action cards for routine, urgent, online, and out-of-hours.
  • Middle of page: supporting detail on consultations, triage, cancellations, and home visits.
  • Lower on page: duty doctor, policy notes, and fuller service guidance.

Why this page works better

It reduces uncertainty. Patients can identify their route quickly, and only then read the detailed process relevant to them.

This is the core UX shift: from “read everything” to “choose your path, then get the detail you need.”
