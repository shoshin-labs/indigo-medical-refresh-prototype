Appointments redesign

Make the right appointment route clear in under ten seconds.

Instead of asking patients to read policy-like paragraphs first, this page turns appointments into a simple decision structure: routine, urgent, online, or out-of-hours.

If the problem is life-threatening, call 999 immediately. If it is urgent and cannot wait for the next routine appointment, call the practice directly on 01534 730541.

Choose the route you need

This is the highest-value change on the whole site: surfacing the correct next step before the policy detail.

1

Routine appointment

For a standard GP or nurse appointment, call reception or visit the surgery. This route should be visible immediately, with no ambiguity about what patients do next.

Contact reception
2

Urgent same-day care

If a patient feels the issue cannot wait, they should be told clearly to call, explain the urgency, and expect triage or a call back from the practice team.

See urgent guidance
3

Online booking

Patients using Patient Access can be guided directly to the online route with a plain-English explanation of what is available and when.

Use Patient Access

Routine consultations

Explain simply that consultations may be in person or by telephone, and that the patient can indicate their preference at the point of booking.

Cancellation policy

Keep cancellation expectations visible, short, and practical so the user understands how to release appointments back to the practice.

Home visits

Clarify that home visits are for housebound or clinically appropriate cases, with a recommended call time if same-day review may be needed.

Out of hours

Surface JDOC guidance higher up the page so patients do not have to hunt for it when anxious or unwell.

What works better here

Lead with action. Layer the detail underneath.

Operational detail still matters, but it becomes more usable when patients see the route first and the supporting explanation second.

  • Top of page: routine, urgent, online, and out-of-hours routes.
  • Mid-page: consultation formats, cancellations, home visits, same-day process.
  • Lower page: fuller policy detail if still required.
Example of sharper copy

Use calmer, shorter language under pressure.

“If your problem is urgent and cannot wait for the next routine appointment, please call us on 01534 730541. Let our reception team know it is urgent so we can triage you appropriately.”

That is faster to absorb than a dense paragraph while preserving the same operational meaning.
