Review for Dr Rogers

A clearer website rather than a louder one.

This page summarises the main website opportunities for Indigo Medical and shows how the concept prototype responds — especially around appointments, contact routes and patient confidence.

What is already strong

Trust and tone

The current Indigo Medical site feels credible, established and reassuring. That warmth should be preserved.

Main issue

Task completion

The site is stronger as an information source than as a task-completion tool. Patients often need to read too much before knowing what to do next.

Highest-value opportunity

Appointments and contact

The biggest practical win is to make routine, urgent, online and out-of-hours routes much clearer and easier to act on.

Summary

What the website should do better

The strongest next step is not a complete rebrand. It is a patient-experience refresh: clearer calls to action, shorter copy in key places, more explicit contact pathways and a more polished explanation of digital services such as Patient Access.

Keep the trust. Reduce the friction.

What this concept shows

How the prototype responds

  • Homepage reorganised around patient intent and practical tasks.
  • Appointments page rewritten as a decision surface rather than explanatory prose.
  • Patient Access page reframed as a simple, reassuring journey.
  • Contact page upgraded with clearer labels and safer routing guidance.
  • Services, surgeries and team pages given a calmer, more polished structure.

Must

Immediate priorities

  • Clarify the appointments journey.
  • Simplify or better explain online access.
  • Make contact routes explicit.
  • Improve forms and accessibility patterns.

Should

Next refinements

  • Rebalance the homepage around booking and urgent guidance.
  • Strengthen mobile scanning and page hierarchy.
  • Add clearer expectations around response times.
  • Introduce more location-specific practical detail.
Overall recommendation

The practice does not need to feel more branded. It needs to feel easier.

A refresh centred on patient tasks, appointment clarity and calmer digital guidance would likely improve usability materially without losing the personal, trusted character already present in the practice brand.
