Presentation-ready review

A sharper digital experience without changing the reassuring character of the practice.

This page turns the earlier notes into a more concise, presentation-ready review: what is already working, where the main opportunities sit, and how the prototype addresses them.

What is already strong

Trust and tone

The current Indigo Medical site feels credible, established, and reassuring. It communicates warmth and professionalism effectively, which matters a great deal for healthcare.

Main issue

Task completion

The site is stronger as an information website than as a task-completion website. Patients often need to read too much before knowing what to do next.

Highest-value opportunity

Appointments

The biggest practical win is to make routine, urgent, online, and out-of-hours appointment routes much clearer and easier to act on.

Short review

What the website should do better

The strongest next step is not a complete rebrand. It is a patient-experience refresh: clearer calls to action, shorter copy in key places, more explicit contact pathways, and a more polished explanation of digital services such as Patient Access.

In short: keep the trust, reduce the friction.

What the prototype demonstrates

How this concept responds

  • Homepage reorganised around patient intent, especially appointments.
  • Appointments page rewritten as a decision surface rather than a block of text.
  • Patient Access onboarding reframed as a simple, reassuring journey.
  • Contact page upgraded with clearer labels and safer routing guidance.
  • Services, surgeries, and team pages expanded into a fuller site concept.

Must

Immediate priorities

  • Clarify the appointment journey.
  • Simplify or better explain online booking.
  • Make contact routes explicit.
  • Improve form usability and accessibility.

Should

Next refinements

  • Rebalance homepage priorities around booking.
  • Surface urgent guidance higher on key pages.
  • Tighten copy, spacing, and content hierarchy.
  • Add realistic response-time expectations.
Overall recommendation

The practice does not need a louder website. It needs a clearer one.

A refresh centred on patient tasks, appointment clarity, and calmer digital guidance would likely improve usability materially without losing the personal, trusted feel already present in the practice brand.
