Executive summary

A calmer, clearer website for patients.

Indigo Medical already has credibility, warmth and strong branding. The main opportunity is not a visual rebrand. It is to make the site much easier for patients to use when they need to book, call, register, order repeat medication or understand where to go next.

In short: keep the trust, reduce the friction, and make the main tasks obvious within a few seconds of landing on the site.

Overall diagnosis

The website is stronger as an information source than a task tool.

A patient usually arrives with a job to do: book, ask, register, request, find, or check. On the current site those tasks are present, but key routes are diluted by long explanatory copy, repeated sections and weak distinctions between urgent, routine and administrative journeys.

This especially affects appointments, contact and digital self-service.

Best outcome

Make the most important actions unmistakable.

The homepage and service pages should orient patients quickly, then let them click straight into the right route. Each key page should answer three questions immediately:

  • What is this page for?
  • What should I do next?
  • What if my situation is urgent or different?

Priority recommendations

These are ordered by likely value to patients and by how much they improve day-to-day usability without requiring a full rebuild.

1
Highest priority

Clarify appointments and urgent routing

Make routine appointments, urgent same-day advice, online access and out-of-hours help visibly different paths. Patients should not need to interpret paragraphs to know what to do.

2
High priority

Separate contact routes more clearly

The site should guide patients to phone for urgent or clinical issues, while keeping forms and email for non-urgent administrative questions.

3
High priority

Make Patient Access and prescriptions easier to understand

Online access is valuable, but the current journey asks patients to work too hard to understand what it does, how to get it and when to use it.

4
Medium priority

Improve scanning on services, surgeries and team pages

These pages contain useful information but can feel dense or generic. They should be reorganised for faster reading and more practical decision-making.

5
Medium priority

Strengthen mobile hierarchy and whitespace

Small structural changes to spacing, card treatment and call-to-action placement will help the site feel calmer and more premium without becoming flashy.

6
Next step

Connect forms and pathways cleanly in production

Once content and structure are agreed, the remaining value comes from wiring forms, online services and booking routes into a secure live implementation.

Page-by-page recommendations

Recommendations below are qualitative, based on reviewing the public site and then building a working static implementation to test the improvements in context.

Homepage

Lead with tasks, not general explanation

  • Surface the most common patient tasks immediately.
  • Make appointments, prescriptions, registration and Patient Access visible above the fold.
  • Reduce duplicate explanatory sections and keep the homepage focused on orientation.
Appointments

Turn the page into a decision surface

  • Split routine, urgent, online and out-of-hours routes clearly.
  • Make the phone route prominent for same-day urgent problems.
  • Keep supporting detail below the main actions rather than before them.
Prescriptions & Patient Access

Explain digital self-service in plain English

  • Show exactly when to use Patient Access.
  • Make repeat prescriptions the simplest route on the page.
  • Explain medication reviews and help routes without jargon.
Registration & contact

Reduce uncertainty before people call reception

  • Tell patients what they need before they start.
  • Make response routes and expectations clearer.
  • Use forms for non-urgent admin only, with strong warnings for urgent issues.
Services & surgeries

Make practical information easier to scan

  • Group services by patient need and keep descriptions short.
  • Highlight real-world details like opening times, parking and which surgery to use.
  • Use strong subheads so visitors can skim with confidence.
Medical team

Add reassurance through people and specialties

  • Show named clinicians in a cleaner structure.
  • Use specialties to help patients understand the breadth of the practice.
  • Keep biographies concise and booking-focused rather than overwhelming.
Key recommendation

The practice does not need to feel more branded. It needs to feel easier.

The strongest route forward is a usability-led refresh: better hierarchy, cleaner calls to action, shorter copy, stronger urgent/routine distinctions and more practical page structures. That can materially improve patient experience without losing the warmth and trust already present in the Indigo Medical brand.

Suggested rollout

Recommended sequence

  • Agree the patient-task structure and page priorities.
  • Approve the content direction for the key high-traffic pages.
  • Translate the approved prototype into the live CMS/build stack.
  • Connect secure forms, Patient Access pathways and any booking integrations.
  • Run a final mobile and accessibility pass before launch.
Evidence note

How to strengthen this further

  • Review analytics for top-traffic pages and common drop-off points.
  • Listen to reception pain points: what patients most often ask because the site was unclear.
  • Run quick patient testing on appointments, prescriptions and contact routes.
  • Use those findings to refine wording, labels and CTAs before launch.

This report is based on qualitative review and prototype work rather than analytics or formal usability testing.
