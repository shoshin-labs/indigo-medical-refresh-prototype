Prescriptions

Medication support without the confusion.

Patients often need to know whether they are ordering a repeat prescription, asking about a medication review, or checking what happens next. Grouping these routes more clearly reduces avoidable calls and uncertainty.

Repeat prescriptions

Make repeat ordering the clearest path

Repeat medication should feel like a straightforward service. The page can direct suitable patients to the fastest route available, including Patient Access where appropriate.

Use Patient Access
New prescriptions

Explain when an appointment is needed

Patients should understand quickly whether a new medication request requires a GP review, a nurse appointment, or another clinician-led follow-up first.

Ordering online

Explain how online repeat requests work and which patients will find this the easiest route.

Medication reviews

Let patients know when medicines need review and when the practice may ask them to book before issuing further repeats.

Queries and timing

Use short guidance on turnaround times, collection expectations and when a medication query is better handled by phone.

Why it matters

A prescription page should lower friction, not add it.

When people are ordering regular medication they want clarity, speed and confidence. Cleaner routes and shorter copy make the service feel more organised and easier to trust.
