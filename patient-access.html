Online access redesign

Explain Patient Access like a service, not a policy document.

The current process can feel administrative and fragmented. This concept turns it into a short, reassuring journey that tells patients what it is, why it helps, what they need, and what happens next.

A

What is it for?

Booking supported appointments, managing repeat prescriptions, and handling routine practice tasks more conveniently online.

B

Who is it for?

Existing patients who want a straightforward digital route for routine tasks and easier access to practice services.

C

What should be clearer?

What “book on the day” means, what ID is needed, how access details are issued, and how long setup is likely to take.

A simpler registration explanation

Even if the operational process stays the same, the experience of understanding it can feel dramatically more modern.

1

Request registration

Offer a clean explanation of how to start, whether via a digital form, downloadable pack, or surgery collection, and what identification is needed.

2

Practice verification

Make it explicit that the practice will verify the request and issue the details needed to activate the account.

3

Complete activation

Show how the patient uses those codes in the app and what they can do once they are signed in.

Reassurance

Replace uncertainty with expectation-setting.

  • Tell patients what they need before they start.
  • Explain what the practice team will do after they submit registration.
  • Describe where to get help if they get stuck.
  • State clearly which appointment types are available online.
Prototype CTA area

One clear next step is better than multiple scattered links.

Patients should not need to puzzle out whether they need a Word document, a policy page, an app store link, or a phone call first. A refreshed page would guide them through that sequence more calmly.
