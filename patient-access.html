Online booking onboarding redesign

Explain Patient Access like a service, not a policy document.

The current journey can feel administrative. This prototype reframes it into a simple, visual setup path that tells patients what the app does, what they need, and what happens next.

What patients need to know first

Before asking someone to register, answer the obvious questions up front.

What is it for?

Book supported appointments, manage repeat prescriptions, and access health information more easily.

Who is it for?

Existing patients who want a more convenient way to manage routine tasks online.

How long does setup take?

Show a realistic expectation, such as a short registration process plus practice verification.

A simpler step-by-step onboarding pattern

Even if the operational requirements stay the same, the presentation can feel far more modern and manageable.

1

Request registration

Offer a clear digital form or downloadable pack, and state exactly what proof of identity is needed.

2

Receive your access details

Explain that the practice will verify registration and send the details needed to activate the account.

3

Complete setup in the app

Guide the patient through final activation and show what they can do once signed in.

Content improvement idea

Replace uncertainty with reassurance

  • What you need
    Photo ID and basic patient details.
  • How long it may take
    Set expectations for verification and activation.
  • Where to get help
    Give one clear route for setup support if patients get stuck.

Example CTA area

Rather than sending patients into multiple paragraphs and document links immediately, start with a simple explanation and a single next step.

Start registration Back to appointments

A later production version could offer a digital registration form or clearer instructions around the current offline verification process.

Prototype focus

Simplify the online-booking explanation and reduce administrative friction.
