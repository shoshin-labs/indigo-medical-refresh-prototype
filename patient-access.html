Patient Access

Online access explained simply.

Patients should be able to understand what Patient Access is for, how to get started, and what to do if they need help — without wading through administrative language.

A

What can I use it for?

Use Patient Access for supported routine tasks such as online appointments and repeat prescriptions where available.

B

Who is it for?

Existing patients who want a digital route for straightforward practice tasks without having to call every time.

C

What should be clearer?

What identification is needed, how registration details are issued, and which appointment types can actually be booked online.

Getting started

A better page tells patients the journey in three steps.

1

Request access

Tell patients whether they need to contact reception, complete a form or collect details from the practice, and what ID to have ready.

2

Receive your registration details

Set clear expectations about what the practice team will send and how long setup is likely to take.

3

Sign in and start using the service

Show what patients can do after activation and where to get help if something does not work first time.

Helpful information

Replace uncertainty with reassurance.

  • Tell patients what they need before they begin.
  • Explain what happens after they submit a request.
  • Show where to get help with access issues.
  • Clarify which tasks are available online and which still need reception support.
Need help?

One clear next step is better than a cluster of mixed links.

If a patient is unsure whether they need a form, an app link or a phone call, the page is doing too little. This version guides them toward a single sensible next action.
